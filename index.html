History of Eiffel Tower

The Famous Building and Most Visited Landmark in Paris

Here's a time line of Eiffel Tower's construction:

  • 1885 - Eiffel presented his plans to the Société des Ingénieurs Civils.
  • 1887 - Eiffel signed it acting in his own capacity.
  • 1887 - East and South legs were built.
  • 1888 - Construction of the upper stage.
  • 1889 - The main structural work was completed.
  • 1925 - The con artist Victor Lustig "sold" the tower for scrap metal.
  • 1926 - Pilot Leon Collet was killed trying to fly under the tower. His aircraft became entangled in an aerial belonging to a wireless station.
  • 1940 - Upon the German occupation of Paris,the lift cables were cut by the French.
  • 1944 - When the Allies were nearing Paris, Hitler ordered General Dietrich von Choltitz, the military governor of Paris, to demolish the tower along with the rest of the city.
  • 1957 - A television antenna was first installed on the tower.
  • 1964 - The Eiffel Tower was officially declared to be a historical monument by the Minister of Cultural Affairs, André Malraux. A year later, an additional lift system was installed in the north pillar.
  • 1990 - The lifts in the east and west legs were replaced by lifts running as far as the second level constructed by the French firm Fives-Lille.
  • 2000 - During the last three minutes of the year, the lights were turned on starting from the base of the tower and continuing to the top to welcome 2000 with a huge fireworks show. An exhibition above a cafeteria on the first floor commemorates this event. The searchlights on top of the tower made it a beacon in Paris's night sky, and 20,000 flashing bulbs gave the tower a sparkly appearance for five minutes every hour on the hour.
